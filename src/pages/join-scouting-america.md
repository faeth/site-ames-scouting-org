Scouts BSA is the next chapter of Scouting, for youth in 6th through 12th grade (typically ages 11&ndash;17). Scouts camp, hike, canoe, and cook their own meals in the outdoors&mdash;and unlike Cub Scouts, the youth are in charge. Scouts elect their own leaders, plan their own campouts, and learn by doing, with adult volunteers there to mentor and keep everyone safe.

New Scouts are welcome at any age. Plenty of youth join in middle school or high school without ever having been a Cub Scout.

## What Scouts do

- Camp year-round, from weekend campouts to week-long summer camp and high-adventure treks
- Earn merit badges in more than 135 subjects, from First Aid and Camping to Robotics and Aviation
- Lead their patrol and troop, building real responsibility and confidence
- Serve the community and work toward Scouting's highest rank: Eagle Scout

## Find a troop

Scouts BSA troops are single-gender, so Ames has troops for boys and troops for girls. We are putting together a guide to each of the Scouts BSA troops in Ames&mdash;check back soon.

In the meantime, you can find every troop near you on Scouting America's official troop finder:

1. Go to [BeAScout.scouting.org](https://beascout.scouting.org/)
2. Choose the **Scouts BSA** program
3. Search ZIP code **50010**

Each troop's listing includes meeting details and a contact form that goes straight to the troop's leaders. They will be glad to invite your family to a meeting so you can see the troop in action before joining.

## Younger than 6th grade?

Kids in Kindergarten through 5th grade start their Scouting adventure in [Cub Scouts](https://amescubscouts.org/join).
